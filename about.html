— The beginning

A travel company built by travellers, not agents.

Nosh Tours began with one observation: India's outbound luxury market was selling packages, not journeys. Travellers were handed pre-cooked itineraries, then quietly billed for transfers, meal upgrades, and surprise activities that should have been disclosed at booking.

Aditya TG and Akshay Manoj decided to build the opposite. A company that handed guests the building blocks — stay tier, pace, regions, experiences, dietary needs — and assembled the itinerary around their choices. Pricing fixed at booking. Everything included. No surcharges, ever.

"You shouldn't need a translator to read your own holiday invoice. That was the entire founding insight."

The first season was bootstrapped on ₹50,000. The second introduced the Nosh Brains model — instead of outsourcing to third-party DMCs, every touchpoint of every tour would be owned by a Nosh Brain in-house. The third year crossed ₹5 crore.
