Day 1–2 Purvakarma — Preparation ▾
Prakriti assessment (40–60 min). Abhyanga synchronized oil massage. Simplified diet begins. You feel pampered. The real work hasn't started yet.
Day 3–4 Oleation Phase ▾
Internal oleation — medicated ghee before dawn. Quantity increases daily. Day four is commonly the hardest. Fatigue, mild nausea, emotional unpredictability. The protocol is working.
Day 5 Virechana — Purgation ▾
The central therapeutic event. Administered by the physician in the morning. By evening you feel lighter in a way that is difficult to describe to someone who hasn't experienced it.
Day 6–7 Paschatkarma — The Reset ▾
Shirodhara. Nasyam. Kizhi. The body is now in its most receptive state. Sleep on night six is commonly the deepest in years. You leave quieter than you arrived.

"Ayurveda is not a treatment.
It is a conversation
between you and your body."

The three doshas — Vata, Pitta, Kapha — are not categories. They are proportions. Every person is a unique combination of all three, and every Nosh physician begins by reading yours before prescribing a single treatment. This is why no two Nosh Kerala protocols are identical, even for guests staying at the same property in the same week.

Vata
Air · Space · Movement

Creative, quick-thinking, light. When imbalanced: anxiety, insomnia, irregular digestion. Protocol focuses on warmth, grounding, and oleation.

Pitta
Fire · Water · Transformation

Sharp, ambitious, focused. When imbalanced: inflammation, irritability, acid reflux. Protocol focuses on cooling, purification, and Virechana.

Kapha
Earth · Water · Structure

Calm, steady, nurturing. When imbalanced: lethargy, weight gain, congestion. Protocol focuses on stimulation, movement, and deep detox.
