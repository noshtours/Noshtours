Hidden Charges

Most "Ayurveda retreats" sold to Western travellers aren't Ayurveda.

They're spa hotels that have added a 60-minute oil massage to the menu and rebranded the wellness suite. The therapists are spa-trained, not medically qualified. There's no consultation, no protocol, no follow-up. The Ayurveda is decorative.

Authentic Kerala Ayurveda is the opposite. It opens with a one-to-two-hour consultation with a BAMS-qualified doctor (the registered medical degree for Ayurveda in India). They prescribe a personalised protocol — a sequence of treatments, dietary plans, and herbal preparations — that runs daily for the length of your stay.

Nosh Tours places UK and US guests only at NABH-certified or government-recognised wellness centres. Somatheeram. Kalari Kovilakom. Niraamaya. Carnoustie. Centres with multi-generation lineages and serious medical oversight. We pre-brief every doctor with your history. We translate where needed. We sit in on the first consultation if you'd like a second pair of ears.

— Questions UK & US guests ask

Frequently Asked

Is Kerala Ayurveda authentic, or is it tourist-grade?
Authentic Kerala Ayurveda is taught by lineages going back centuries and is practised by registered BAMS-qualified doctors at certified centres. Nosh Tours places guests only at NABH-certified or government-recognised wellness centres — not at spa hotels that brand "Ayurveda massage" as a marketing layer.
What's the best time of year for a Kerala Ayurveda retreat?
Counter-intuitively, the monsoon season (June to September) is considered the most therapeutic period in classical Ayurveda — the cooler humid climate is believed to open the body's channels and improve treatment efficacy. We run dedicated monsoon programmes.
How long should a meaningful retreat be?
A pure rejuvenation programme works in 7–14 days. Full Panchakarma typically requires 14–21 days. Shorter trips can deliver rest but not therapeutic depth. We never sell 3-night "Ayurveda experiences."
Are practitioners and front-of-house staff fluent in English?
Yes. At every centre we partner with, the doctor, primary practitioner, and front-of-house team speak fluent English. We pre-brief them with your medical history and intent so the first consultation arrives prepared.
How are payments handled — INR conversion or fixed GBP/USD?
All wellness packages are quoted in GBP and USD with fixed prices at booking. You pay in your home currency. No exchange-rate volatility, no surprise INR conversion charges.
What if I have a chronic condition — is this medical or wellness?
Ayurveda is a registered medical system in India under the AYUSH ministry. However, our retreats are positioned as wellness-led, not medical treatment. We always recommend continuing your home physician's care plan in parallel and disclosing all conditions during the pre-consultation.
